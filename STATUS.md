## Built

- **Core (`core/`, header-only):** phase table, substance, settling, separation (Tromp), magnetic, smelt (bloomery + copper/tin furnace + roast), forge (consolidate + shape + cast + alloy), levigate (+ flocculate), fire, geology (spatial field; the pick gate — the deep rock is a competence wall only a concentrated blow gets under), fuel (wood → pyrolysis → charcoal; the two-gate finding; fire tiers), firekit (the ignition ladder + the smolder kit; the spark is a made tool, not a free match), char (the charcoal-pit transform; the seal is the gate), knap (stone → edge; fracture is the gate; the edge's irreducible floor), haft (edge + stick → the first tool; the haft is force, the joint is the ceiling), split (felled log → billets; the wedge not the edge — the grain splits the log), season (green billet → air-dry stock; the calendar, not the tool; drying time grows with thickness²), dress (seasoned billet → the timber haft; the adze trues the seat — one edge hafted twice, a wedge and a calendar between).
- **Chain:** dig → carry → break → pan → furnace, end to end. Three metals (iron, copper, tin) + one alloy (bronze); both faces of the sulfur problem (red-short in iron, roast in copper).
- **Playable slices (11 TUs):** pan, dig, breaker, furnace, carry, char, gather, knap, haft. `ratchet` is an instrument, not a slice.

## Settled findings

One line each; full statements in `DESIGN.md`, reversals in `CORRECTIONS.md`.

- Progression is a staircase, not a ratchet.
- Levigation authors nothing (imperfection = 3.0 exactly).
- Levigation divides the clay bin (6.977×) — no lodestone-shaped hole.
- Throughput is a bigger shovel; a bigger pot is progression.
- Fluxless bloomery smelts only at Fe/SiO₂ ≥ 1.859 — the pan alone cannot make smeltable ore.
- A wrought bar's floor is connectivity (~1.5%), not effort.
- Form is free at any heat; hardness is only cold.
- The contaminant no separator sees (sulfur) breaks the tool (red-short).
- Abundance is not the gate; melting point is (copper pours, iron sponges).
- Alloying is not averaging — bronze lands off the end of both parents.
- Sulfur: cured at the furnace (copper roast) or hidden to the anvil (iron).
- A stony floor swallows the feed (shelter); it does not blur it.
- Co-location is the gate, now geometry.
- `roast()` cures only the sulfide the crushing freed — the recovery ceiling is set at the breaker.
- Charcoal is the reductant, not merely a hotter fuel — a wood fire clears the reduction onset and still smelts nothing.
- A fire has two independent gates: reductant (carbon in the charge, chemical) and peak temperature (fuel × air, thermal).
- A charcoal pit works by suffocating its own fire — open it to the air and it burns the char to ash; the seal, not the wood, sets the yield.
- Gathering by hand wins tinder and sticks, never timber — the bulk fuel and every haft are locked behind the axe (hands are tool #1, the worst point on the curve).
- The reductant a copper smelt needs is tiny — a small pit of hand-gathered sticks covers it many times over; charcoal binds the fire's heat (and the bloom's blast), not the copper reduction. The furnace burns made charcoal now, not a free bed.
- Firemaking is a kit, not a minigame — and the spark is a made TOOL, not a free match (`firekit.h`). The labor is gathering the rungs (tinder, sticks, and — post-axe — split timber); the player assembles the kit and pushes it into the station, and the lighting GESTURE is glossed off-screen like the panner's wrist. But the SPARK is not: `can_light()` spawned it, and firekit.h stops it. The smolder kit (`SmolderKit`, `make_smolder`) is a persistent, wear-free friction set carved from dry dead wood; possession is the gate, so a loaded station is inert storage until the player strikes it (`ignite`). The ignition ladder (`fire_tier`) — no spark catches a log, each rung lights the next, a gap stops the climb — governs what the kit *can* contain per era (a smelt-grade timber fire is locked until the axe frees timber), a strict bottom-up ordering that authors no number. Same shape as the haft and the pick: new tech adds a rung, never retires the ones under it; possession, not a gesture, is the gate. The kit's haul mass (`kit_mass`) is its rungs summed, hauled under carry.cpp's caps — carry limit is slice-local pacing, not a number here.
- A stone edge is gated by fracture, not hardness — the tough stone underfoot is a hammerstone; only a conchoidal core takes an edge, and even a clean flake is only as keen as the stone floor, a wall the next material (metal), not effort, gets under. Knapping authors nothing: the edge is subtracted, mass conserved.
- Hafting is force, not sharpness — it puts knap's floored edge on a lever, and a hafted axe fells the timber a bare hand and bare edge cannot, with an edge that never got keener. The joint (bind × stock) is the weakest link and the ceiling; the first axe must ride a sapling haft (timber is locked behind it), so the bootstrap turns exactly once: hands → stone edge → sapling axe → felled trunk → real haft.
- A felled trunk is not a haft, and the axe that felled it cannot make it one. Felling is force (axe, `EDGE_ACROSS`, severs the standing grain — the sapling swing is enough); the finishing is the edge (adze, `EDGE_INLINE`, pares along the grain, no fell-wall behind it). So the bootstrap turns on one knapped edge hafted TWICE — axe to win the trunk, adze to true the haft — with the same crude sapling kit, because the edge trues where the lever fells.
- Between the axe's two haftings sit two EDGELESS stations, and the log passes all three verbs before it is a haft. **Split** (`split.h`): a felled round log is riven into billets by a WEDGE, not an edge — the grain splits the log, you only open it, so a billet along the grain keeps full-length fibres (stiff) and one across snaps (waste). It can be done before the adze exists. **Season** (`season.h`): a green billet is three-quarters water, and only TIME dries it — the first station gated by the calendar, not force/edge/geometry. Nothing stiffens until the wood falls below fibre saturation; drying time grows with the SQUARE of thickness (split thin or wait a lifetime — a 20 mm billet air-dries in ~1 yr, a whole log never). A head bound green loosens as it dries: the joint fails at the calendar. Seasoning is what EARNS `TIMBER`'s full hold — `dress(stave, adze)` yields timber only for a billet BOTH riven along the grain AND seasoned to air-dry; miss either and it hands back the `SAPLING`. So the split+season detour is what actually justifies the timber joint the sapling never could seat.
- The panner's tilt is assumed, not played. The game glosses every motor skill (the axe swing, the knapping strike); the wrist is no different — mastering it teaches nothing about why the technology works or how it evolved, and a live tilt knob is a tax, not a decision. So the tilt is earned off-screen; the skill is relocated from hands to head — the player's wash/passes/stop and re-pan-or-dump decisions, graded by the settling cut. Failure survives (over-wash loses ore over the lip; stop early and it never stratifies), and the historical lesson is the pan's ceiling (zero-tool entry, blind to locked/fine ore), not the gesture.
- The pick is REACH, not only rate. Weathering leaves the oxide cap friable enough for bare hands, but the sulfide root is fresh competent rock a hand skips off — only the pick's concentrated blow (`haft.h` bite, force not keenness) gets under it. So the primary ore, and roast-grade copper, is gated behind the whole tool staircase; a placer the river already broke needs no pick at all. On any tier both tools open, the makeup is identical (`sample()` is tool-blind) — the tool changes reach and rate, never makeup. This makes "the gossans were worked before the deep sulfides" mechanical, and closes the largest loop in the project: the staircase (gather→knap→haft→pick) was climbed to get back *under* the ground the chain started on. The pick is an access step, not a verb: there is no pick-minigame — you make the tool, take it up (`dig` `[t]`), and the deeper competent rock opens. Reach is the gate (belongs on possession); the swing itself carries no decision, so it stays a tool toggle inside the dig verb, never its own gesture.
